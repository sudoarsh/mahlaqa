Bibliography & Links

Primary Witnesses, Methodological Precedents, and Literature

Primary Sources (Manuscripts & Early Prints)

  • British Library IO Islamic 2768 [BL 1799]. Diwan-e-Chanda. Presentation copy gifted to James Kirkpatrick. Contains 97 ghazals. Persian preface by Syed Naseeruddin Khan Qudrat. Extant at the British Library, London.
  • Telangana Oriental MS 228 [TO 1811]. Diwan-e-Chanda. Circle copy containing 98 ghazals and Persian preface. Extant at the Telangana Oriental Manuscript Library, Hyderabad.
  • Salar Jung Museum Manuscript [SJ 1818]. Diwan-e-Chanda, appended to the Tarikh-e-Dil-Afroz. Base text for this critical edition. Contains 125 ghazals. Extant at the Salar Jung Museum, Hyderabad.
  • University of Pennsylvania Oversize LJS 63 [UPenn 1804]. Khushhal Khan "Anoop". Rāg darshan. Illustrated manuscript commissioned by Raja Rao Ranbha Bahadur. Held at the Kislak Center for Special Collections, Rare Books and Manuscripts, University of Pennsylvania. View at OpenNN ↗
  • Gulzar-e-Mah Laqa [GM 1906]. Lithograph print edition of the Diwan-e-Chanda. Hyderabad, 1906. Contains 125 ghazals. The primary source of the 20th-century textus receptus.

Secondary Literature & Historical Context

  • Bilgrami, Syed Ali Asgar. Landmarks of the Deccan: A Comprehensive Guide to the Archaeological Remains of the City and Suburbs of Hyderabad. Government Central Press, Hyderabad-Deccan, 1927.
  • Kugle, Scott. When Sun Meets Moon: Gender, Eros, and Ecstasy in Urdu Poetry. University of North Carolina Press, 2016.
  • Jauhar Bidri, Ghulam Husain Khan. Tajalliyat-e-Mah Laqa. (Various manuscript copies detailing the history of the Asaf Jahi dynasty and containing encomiums for Mah Laqa Bai).
  • Tiwari, Akash. Sher Behtar Kaise Hua? Rekhta Publications, 2025.

Methodological References in Digital Philology

  • Gabler, Hans Walter. Ulysses: A Critical and Synoptic Edition. Garland Publishing, 1984. (Methodological precedent for mapping editorial interference and creating a synoptic apparatus).
  • Johnson, Thomas H. The Poems of Emily Dickinson: Including variant readings critically compared with all known manuscripts. Belknap Press of Harvard University Press, 1955. (Methodological precedent for bypassing corrupted printed textus receptus to restore authorial manuscript orthography).
  • Khaleghi-Motlagh, Djalal (ed.). The Shahnameh (The Book of Kings). Persian Heritage Foundation, 1988–2008. (Application of the Lachmannian method in the Perso-Arabic manuscript tradition).
  • Huson, Daniel H., and David Bryant. "Application of Phylogenetic Networks in Evolutionary Studies." Molecular Biology and Evolution, vol. 23, no. 2, 2006, pp. 254-267. (Reference for SplitsTree algorithms used in the cladistic analysis of manuscript transmission).
