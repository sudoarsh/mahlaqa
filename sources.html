Codicological Survey

Establishing the Hierarchy of Truth in the Deccani Tradition

Salar Jung (1818) SJ

Role: Matn-e-Asas (The Base Text)

Commissioned just six years prior to her death, this manuscript represents Mah Laqa Bai's final authorial intention. Containing 125 ghazals, it exhibits highly stable orthography and preserves the pure Deccani dialect. Despite minor scribal errors, its supreme authorial authority makes it the anchor line for our structural alignment.

British Library (1799) BL

Role: Primary Genetic Witness

An early authorial presentation copy containing 97 ghazals, originally gifted to British Resident Sir John Malcolm. It acts as an invaluable document for tracking early variants and repairing physical lacunae in SJ. Crucially, it preserves a detailed Persian Dibacha proving the Diwan was a state-sanctioned project.

Telangana Archives (1811) TO

Role: Intermediate Witness

Containing 98 ghazals, this manuscript acts as a chronological bridge. Its textual harmony with the 1799 London manuscript definitively proves that Mah Laqa Bai's early Deccani poetic tradition remained remarkably stable for over a decade before the final 1818 compilation.

Print Edition (1906) GM

Role: The Corrupted Tradition

Published anonymously in Hyderabad, this print edition is a direct but heavily corrupted derivative of SJ. The 1906 editor systematically replaced archaic Deccani vocabulary, stripped away cultural references, and altered meters to forcefully fit the poetry into early 20th-century standard Northern Urdu grammar.
