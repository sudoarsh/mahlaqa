Rediscovering Mah Laqa

دیوانِ چندا — تنقیدی ایڈیشن

The Poetess of the Deccan

Mah Laqa Bai Chanda (c. 1768–1820) was the undisputed luminary of the Asaf Jahi court. As the first woman to compile a complete Diwan in Urdu, she carved a space for female authorship within a fiercely patriarchal literary tradition. She was not merely a poet, but a Renaissance woman of the Deccan: an elite equestrian, a master of Hindustani classical music trained by the lineage of Tansen, and a formidable political diplomat. Her life and work represent the dazzling pinnacle of the 18th-century cultural synthesis.

The Digital Critical Edition

For over 120 years, modern scholarship has misread her poetic evolution. This project performs a historical rescue mission. By conducting a line-by-line, word-level collation of the surviving primary manuscripts spanning twenty years of her career, this edition proves her original authorial voice was remarkably stable.

The heavy variations and linguistic normalizations previously attributed to her are actually late textual corruptions introduced by an anonymous 1906 print editor. This digital companion to the print edition restores her authentic Deccani text from manuscript evidence for the first time since her death.
